Welcome to Summer Design School 2018!

We are so excited for you to join us for a week long learning experience!
We are sending a few attachments in preparation for Monday’s Spring Design School kick-off:

  1. The Prepackage information will inform you on the schedule, dress code and other frequently asked questions. Please note the transportation to the different sites is your responsibility.
  2. The Wall of Problems Preparation assignment (we won’t call it homework…). Ana Monroe and Aaron Stienstra will be leading that part on Monday, and have prepared the enclosed document for you. We hope you will have some time before Monday to respond to the prompts. Aaron and Ana will be holding a webinar session this Wednesday (March 14th) from 12:00-12:45 pm EST to talk through this.
  3. The Pre-Event Survey. Please email it to Lab@opm.gov or print and bring along with you on Monday. We will be collecting these surveys at the registration table when you come in. These surveys are very important because they will represent the baseline from which we will be able to assess the overall workshop.
  4. The Photography and Video Consent Form will allow us to properly document this event for our records and for the benefit of future iterations. Please print and bring along with you on Monday. We’ll collect it at the registration table as well.

Our first morning (Monday March 19) will be hosted by the National Endowment for the Arts, located at 400 7th Street SW, in the Constitution Center. The building is directly above L’Enfant Plaza metro, 7th and D Street entrance to the station. Please use the D Street/metro entrance rather than the entrance on 7th Street; we will have a table set up for registration in the lobby.

Very important: please bring your federal ID/PIV card badge, which will speed along security screening. A driver’s license or other government-issued ID is also acceptable.

Please plan to arrive by 8:45 to clear security; we will start at 9:00 in Conference Room C on the ground floor of the Constitution Center. The building has a cafeteria with full breakfast options available for purchase. Food and drink are also allowed in the room where we will be meeting.

In case you need them, here are more detailed directions for navigating L’Enfant Plaza Station:

  1. Take the orange/blue/silver or green/yellow lines to L’Enfant Plaza metro stop. Exit through the D & 7th streets exit.
  2. You will see a William Wegman portrait of a waving astronaut dog against a black background at the exit. (There is another astronaut dog against a white background elsewhere in the station; you want the dog with the black background.) Go through the exit gates and walk under the dog to the exit escalator. Take the escalator to the top.
  3. When you reach the top of the metro escalator, turn left, where you will see the entrance to the Constitution Center.

After Monday, we will meet at the Lab at OPM, in the sub-basement of the OPM building, located on 1900 E Street NW, between 19th and 20th streets. Please access the building through the Visitor’s entrance on E St. Building security will have a full list of participants to speed up the entry process, and you will also need your federal ID/PIV card badge every day.
